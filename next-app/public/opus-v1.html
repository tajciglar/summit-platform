II.

An Unhurried Answer to an Overwhelming Question

We built the ADHD Parenting Summit because we could not find the thing we needed: a generous, evidence-based conversation that treats ADHD parenting as something other than a problem to be fixed. IV.

Six Transformations, One Week.

What the end of Day Five may sound like at your kitchen table.

  1. I.

    Understand Why They Act Out

    The neuroscience behind the behavior — so "bad child" becomes "dysregulated nervous system," and the response changes.

  2. II.

    End the Yelling Cycle

    Calm responses that actually work — not because you found willpower, but because you found a different script.

  3. III.

    Fix Morning Chaos

    A small collection of routines that reduce daily friction. No charts with gold stars. Things that hold up in real kitchens.

  4. IV.

    Build Emotional Resilience

    Help your child regulate big emotions — not suppress them. The difference, over time, changes everything.

  5. V.

    Navigate School Systems

    IEPs, 504s, and the quiet art of teacher communication. Language that is firm, warm, and hard to refuse.

  6. VI.

    Connect With Other Parents

    A quiet side-channel for those late-night questions. You are not alone in this; most parents in our rooms have felt exactly what you feel.

X.

Questions, Answered

Is it really free?
Yes, entirely. All forty sessions, the supplement, and the three bonus sidebars. You'll receive upgrade offers later — these are optional and never gate the free content.
What if I miss a live session?
Each session is recorded and available for 48 hours after it airs. Registered readers with the All-Access Pass keep permanent access to every recording.
Is this suitable if my child isn't formally diagnosed?
Absolutely. Many of our attendees are observing ADHD traits and deciding whether to pursue assessment. The content is relevant regardless of diagnostic status.
My partner wants to attend too — do they need a separate seat?
One registration covers your household. Bring your partner, grandparents, co-parent, or an older child if appropriate.
Is medical advice offered?
Our contributors are qualified clinicians, but the summit is educational, not clinical. We'll point you toward where to go for personalized medical guidance.
