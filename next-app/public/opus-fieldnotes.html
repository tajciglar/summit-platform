Method · what we do here

§ 01

We opened this Register because the good parenting books teach philosophy and the bad ones teach compliance. Neither works when your eight-year-old is frozen in the hallway and the bus is in four minutes.

Every session answers three questions, in order: what is happening in their nervous system, what words do I use, and what do I do when those words stop working?

That is the entire brief we give every contributor. It is why people come back. It is why 73,124 parents attended in 2025 — and why, of them, 31% arrived pre-diagnosis and found the register still useful.

— N. Ibarra-Schmidt, curator, the seventh Register

Questions — asked and answered

§ 05

Five questions we're asked most before each Register opens.

Q.01 Is it really free? +
Yes. Live attendance and same-day replays are free. A lifetime-access pass with transcripts, mp3s and session downloads is offered for $47 after you register — genuinely optional, never pushed mid-session. About 14% of attendees take it.
Q.02 What time are sessions, and what if I miss one? +
Live Tuesday through Saturday, 19:00 – 21:30 Eastern. Each evening's replay goes live by 10:00 the following morning and stays accessible for 48 hours.
Q.03 My child hasn't been formally diagnosed. Still for me? +
Yes. Roughly 31% of attendees arrive pre-diagnosis. Day II opens with "should we pursue an evaluation, and if so, how" — including what to bring and what to expect to pay.
Q.04 Is any of this medication advice? +
No. We cover the clinical landscape and the questions to take to your prescriber. Specific decisions stay with you and your pediatrician — a boundary every contributor is briefed on before they speak.
Q.05 Can I watch with my partner? +
Yes — one registration per household covers both of you. About 28% of attendees do it as a couple, which tends (anecdotally) to produce the fastest Monday-morning shifts.
