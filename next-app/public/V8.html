Ch. 10 Why This Matters

Why this is more important

Than ever before…

Fact 01Ref [1]

ADHD is the top mental or developmental condition among children.[1]

Fact 02Ref [2]

Every 3rd child with ADHD has symptoms that last into adulthood.[2]

Fact 03Ref [3]

A child with ADHD has a 1 in 4 chance of having a parent who also has it.[3]

Fact 04Ref [4]

Children with ADHD are 2–3× more likely to develop substance abuse or addiction.[4]

Fact 05Ref [5]

Children with ADHD are more likely to develop low self-esteem, anxiety, and depression.[5]

Fact 06Ref [6]

Compared to peers, children with ADHD earn significantly lower school grades.[6]

But here’s the good news: the earlier you learn how to support your ADHD child, the better your chances of managing their condition and minimizing long-term issues.

FAQ

When is the Summit happening? +
The ADHD Parenting Summit 2026 runs for five evenings, Tuesday February 10 through Saturday February 14, 2026. Each evening’s sessions begin at 9 PM CET / 3 PM ET.

Yes. It’s a free online event accessible from anywhere in the world. If you can’t watch live, you can upgrade to a VIP Pass for lifetime access.
How can I watch the recordings? +
You’ll receive links two days before the Summit begins. Sessions unlock automatically each evening and are free to watch for 24 hours.
I’m busy during the summit. Can I still watch the videos? +
Each day’s recordings are available for 24 hours. For unlimited lifetime access, upgrade to our VIP Pass.

No, sessions are pre-recorded and released simultaneously each day. You’ll have free access to the videos for 24 hours after release.

Each evening features 6–9 sessions, each 30–60 minutes. All sessions air at the same time each day.

All sessions are pre-recorded. During the 24-hour viewing window, you can watch any speaker from that day at your own pace.
