§ II — A note from the curator

p. 06

Most parenting content is beautifully written advice you cannot use on a Tuesday at 7:41 AM.

We made this summit because the good parenting books teach you philosophy and the bad ones teach you compliance. Neither helps when your eight-year-old is frozen in the hallway and the bus is in four minutes.

Every session answers three questions in this order: What is actually happening in their nervous system? What specific words do I use? What do I do when those words stop working? That is the whole brief we give every contributor. It's why people come back.

— N. Ibarra-Schmidt, curator, the seventh Register

§ V — Answers to readers' questions

p. 24

The five most-asked questions, answered.

Is it really free?
Yes. Live attendance and same-day replays are free. A lifetime-access pass with transcripts, mp3s and session downloads is offered for $47 after you register — genuinely optional, never pushed mid-session. About 14% of attendees take it.
What time are sessions, and what if I miss one?
Live Tuesday through Saturday, 7:00 – 9:30 PM Eastern. Each evening's replay goes live by 10 AM the following day and stays accessible for 48 hours.
My child hasn't been formally diagnosed. Still for me?
Yes. Roughly 31% of attendees arrive pre-diagnosis. Day II opens with a session on "should we pursue an evaluation, and if so, how" — including what to bring and what to expect to pay.
Is any of this medication advice?
No. We cover the clinical landscape and the questions to take to your prescriber. Specific decisions stay with you and your pediatrician — a boundary every contributor is briefed on before they speak.
Can I watch with my partner?
Yes — one registration per household covers both of you. About 28% of attendees do it as a couple, which tends (anecdotally) to produce the fastest Monday-morning shifts.
