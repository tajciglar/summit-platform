# Design Context — landing-pages-wcc

## Users

**Primary**: Parents of kids with ADHD, ages 5-17. High comorbidity — roughly 74% are ADHD themselves (inherited). Time-blind, executive-function challenged, frequently overwhelmed, often sensory-sensitive. Evening readers with limited focus budget. Mixed formal-diagnosis status — ~31% arrive pre-diagnosis.

**Context of use**: On a phone after the kids are down, during a fractured 15-minute window. They've either just read something alarming from a teacher, had a bad meltdown, or someone at a parent group mentioned the summit. Low patience for jargon, zero patience for marketing fluff.

**Job to be done**: "Show me — in under 30 seconds — that you will help me not yell at my kid tomorrow, that you're not selling me a scam, and that I don't have to pay."

**Outcome**: Free email registration via a popup modal (NOT an inline form at the bottom — GLOBAL RULE).

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: *quietly authoritative · warm · uncondescending.*

- **Voice**: Like a therapist who writes well — specific, honest, not breezy. Uses real examples ("the shoe card on the counter") rather than benefits ("transform your family!"). Never "unlock", "elevate", "journey", "transform".
- **Evidence-led**: Every claim traces to a clinician, study, or mechanism. Organic numbers (`73,124`, `4.87/5`, `312%`, `0.83 confidence`) instead of rounded marketing numbers (`50K+`, `99.9%`).
- **Anti-apologetic**: Talks to parents as adults who are already doing hard things. Never "you might feel like a bad parent" or "it's okay to..."
- **Legal / clinical boundaries are explicit**: "No. We cover the clinical landscape and questions to ask your prescriber — specific decisions stay with you and your pediatrician."

## Aesthetic Direction

**Editorial, not marketing.** Closer to *The Atlantic* or *Monocle* than SaaS landing pages.

**Theme mode**: **Light only — or light-cream / warm parchment.** No dark-mode variants. Dark CTA/inverted sections inside a light page are OK if restrained.

**Color direction** (any variant may choose one of):
- Warm cream (`#faf8f4` / `#f4ecd9`) + deep rose (`#b1344a`)
- Warm paper (`#faf6ed`) + rust/terracotta (`#a84e2a`)
- Sage-washed neutral + terracotta accent
- Parchment + butter yellow (`#9a6a14` on `#f4ecd9`)
- Bone + deep teal

**Single accent rule**: Max one accent per variant, saturation ≤ 80%.

**Anti-references** — what this must NOT look like:
- AI purple / cyan-on-dark / indigo gradients
- Glassmorphism / frosted-glass cards / neon glows
- 3-column feature-card rows with icon + heading + paragraph
- Hero with three rounded-rect stats + gradient number
- Gradient text on headings or stats
- Rounded-rect cards with generic drop shadows
- Inter as body font
- Pure black (`#000`) or pure white (`#fff`)
- Generic stock-headshot speaker grids
- "Unleash", "transform", "elevate", "your journey"
- Countdown timers on optin pages (timers are sales-only — GLOBAL RULE)

**Typography palette** (pick one pairing per variant):
- Geist + Instrument Serif italic
- Outfit + Fraunces
- Cabinet Grotesk + Fraunces + Geist Mono

## Design Principles

1. **Legibility over stimulation.** ADHD audience. Hero dates visible at a glance, body copy high-contrast, tiny tracked-out uppercase eyebrows are a last resort — prefer 12-13px + modest tracking. Motion is near-zero (press marquee exempt); sensory-sensitive readers should not feel hijacked.

2. **Specificity beats persuasion.** A named speaker at "Tue 10 Feb · 7:00 PM ET · 45m" outperforms "40+ world-class experts". Every claim pins a time, a number, or a name. Don't write for impact — write for recognition ("that's me, my house, my kid").

3. **No photos; refined typographic placeholders.** No stock portraits. Use two-letter monograms (`NO`, `TI`, `FO`) in muted tinted squares/circles, or large typographic marks, or geometric plates. Never "egg" SVG avatars or generic Lucide user icons.

4. **Editorial rhythm, not card grids.** Prefer numbered lists, indexed tables of contents, 2-col zig-zag, bento with asymmetric weights. Never 3-col card rows. Vary spacing — tight stacks around related content, generous breath around CTAs and section breaks. Left-aligned prose blocks over centered ones.

5. **Hero dates are structural, not incidental.** A visible 3-cell `Opens · Closes · Live` date block (or equivalent) belongs in the hero above the fold, not in 11px eyebrow mono. Event dates are the product's promise.

6. **One CTA per section, same word every time.** "Claim your free seat" throughout — not "Register" / "Reserve" / "Get access" cycling. Lower executive-function cost; reinforces the single decision.

7. **Respect** `prefers-reduced-motion` **aggressively.** Marquee keeps running (it's part of the "press strip" pattern — GLOBAL RULE), but staggered reveals, pulse dots, floating badges all stop. No parallax, no scroll-hijacking.

## Tech & Constraints

- **Static HTML** — Tailwind via CDN, inline `<style>`, vanilla JS only for the optin modal. No React/Next in these variants (the Next app exists but these are raw HTML deliverables served from `public/`).
- Target viewports: 375 / 768 / 1280 / 1440. Touch targets ≥ 44×44.
- WCAG AA minimum on body text (7:1 preferred on uppercase eyebrow text).
- No external image hosts (no Unsplash, no pravatar, no picsum). Decorative shapes via inline SVG.
- Single optin modal pattern, reusable across variants (`body.modal-open` lock, ESC close, backdrop close).
- Marquee exempt from motion cuts but honors `prefers-reduced-motion` at the OS layer.

## Audit targets

Score ≥ 17/20 before shipping:
- Accessibility ≥ 3 (WCAG AA body + eyebrow, 44px touch targets, focus rings, semantic headings)
- Performance ≥ 3 (no layout thrash, transform/opacity only, `loading="lazy"` where applicable — though these are mostly no-image)
- Responsive ≥ 3 (375/768/1280 all clean, no horizontal scroll)
- Theming ≥ 3 (CSS custom properties, not hard-coded hex in markup)
- Anti-Patterns = 4 (zero AI-slop tells; pass the "could this be an AI made this?" test)
